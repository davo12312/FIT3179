US Police Shootings 2015-2020

In recent events, fatal police shootings as well as police brutality has become a matter of contention within the United States of America. As this controversial topic has giving rise to civil movements such as Black Lives Matter, we can take a closer look at what police shootings have occured from 2015 to 2020. This visualisation will show an overview of police shootings as well as delve into the distribution of shootings based on location, ethnicity and age.

Police Shootings In Each State

When we take a look at the number of fatal police shootings per capita of each state in the USA we can see that New Mexico and Alaska have the highest numbers at 4.44 shootings and 4.92 shootings in the past 5 years respectively. The states with the lowest is New York with 0.46 police shootings per 100,000 people and Rhode Island with 0.38 people.

An interesting point to look at is the total number of fatal police shootings from the 5 years. For example, California has the highest number of total police shootings at 701 people which is 275 more people than Texas which has the second highest total at 426 people. However, as both states have the two highest populations, once normalised their shootings per capita becomes 1.77 (California) and 1.47 (Texas).

Ethnicity of Shootings

Taking a closer look at the different enthnicities in each state and the number of shootings within those ethnicities, we see that within a majority of states, white people are involved in most of the fatal police shootings in each state. They are followed by black, then hispanic, natives and asians in that order.

From what we can see in the scatterplot, there is no apparent pattern to the number of shootings and the ethnicity population in each state. However, from what we can see, it appears as though hispanic people in New Mexico and white people in Alaska have the highest involvemnt in fatal police shootings per capita at 3.05 and 3.01 respectively.
